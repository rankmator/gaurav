Our Painless 4-Step Protocol

Experience how digital planning and ergonomic precision transform implantology into a relaxing journey.

01

3D CBCT Digital Diagnostic Scan

We capture ultra-precise 3D scans of your jawbone density and nerve canals. Using specialized software, Dr. Mathpal plans exact insertion angles with sub-millimeter digital surgical guides.

02

Micro-Surgical Guided Placement

Under local anesthesia and computer-guided mapping, the biocompatible titanium post is placed smoothly into the jawbone. The minimally invasive technique ensures zero pain and rapid recovery.

03

Biological Osseointegration

Over the next few weeks, your natural bone fuses seamlessly around the biocompatible titanium root in a biological process called osseointegration, creating a rock-solid foundation.

04

Custom Zirconia Crown Attachment

Once fully fused, a bespoke, multi-layered zirconia prosthetic crown crafted in our digital lab is securely attached. You walk out with a stunning, durable, and completely natural smile.

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear, honest answers to help you make an informed decision about your oral health.

Is the dental implant surgery painful?
+
Not at all. With Dr. Gaurav Mathpal's commitment to 100% Painless Dentistry, the procedure is performed under advanced local anesthesia. Most patients report feeling much less discomfort during implant placement than during a simple tooth extraction. Post-operative recovery is remarkably smooth with minimal tenderness.
How long do dental implants last?
+
Dental implants are engineered as a permanent, lifelong solution. While natural teeth are susceptible to decay and cavities, medical-grade titanium implants cannot decay. With regular brushing, flossing, and bi-annual dental check-ups, your implant post can easily last a lifetime.
Am I a candidate if I have diabetes or bone loss?
+
Yes! Controlled diabetes is rarely a barrier to successful implant placement. For patients who have experienced significant bone loss due to long-term missing teeth, Dr. Mathpal performs specialized bone grafting or bone ridge preservation protocols prior to or during placement to ensure solid support.
What is the difference between an implant and a traditional bridge?
+
A traditional bridge requires grinding down healthy enamel from the two adjacent teeth to support the false tooth, and it does not stimulate the underlying jawbone. An implant replaces the root directly without touching or harming adjacent healthy teeth, preserving your natural facial bone structure permanently.
